Key Analysis Page1

As a market research analyst in the Saudi tourism industry in 2022, I unraveled captivating insights into the dynamics of tourism in Saudi Arabia. Through meticulous analysis, a pattern emerged revealing the seasonal fluctuations in tourism visits, encompassing both Saudi and non-Saudi travelers. Peaks were observed in July and December, with a decline in March, coinciding with the Saudi students' exam periods.

Delving deeper, it was discovered that 35% of tourists flock to Saudi Arabia for entertainment purposes, while a significant majority of 60% visit to reunite with friends and family. Furthermore, the research highlighted a strong correlation between tourism spending and the influx of visitors, as well as the availability of accommodation options. An intriguing trend emerged, showcasing a steady increase in the number of Saudis venturing abroad from July to December.

Key Analysis Page2

In the above dashboard, it is evident that the most visited and highly spending destinations for both Saudi and non-Saudi tourists are Makkah and Riyadh. A noteworthy observation from the data is that during the period from January to May, there is a decline in travel for entertainment purposes by both Saudi and non-Saudi individuals. However, starting from May, the numbers gradually increase month by month until January, indicating a surge in entertainment-related travel.

Upon further analysis, it can be inferred that this trend is influenced by the events schedule and the unfavorable sea conditions during the winter season. The lack of suitable sea-related activities during this period might deter tourists from seeking entertainment in coastal areas.

Key Analysis Page3

In the previous dashboard, it is evident that the top three destinations for non-Saudi tourists are Bahrain, UAE, and Egypt, while for Saudi tourists, the top three destinations are Egypt, UAE, and Yemen. Furthermore, when examining the top tourism countries arriving in Saudi Arabia, the notable countries are Kuwait, India, and Egypt.

Upon further investigation, it becomes apparent that a significant portion of tourism from India, Pakistan, and Egypt to Saudi Arabia is driven by individuals searching for job opportunities.

Market research in the tourism industry can benefit tourism boards, hospitality providers, tour operators, government organizations, and investors. It helps tourism boards understand visitor preferences and develop effective marketing strategies. Hospitality providers can tailor their offerings based on tourist needs. Tour operators gain insights into popular destinations and travel trends.
