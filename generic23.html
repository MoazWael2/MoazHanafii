Key Analysis

The analysis reveals a staggering number of almost 5 million crimes committed against women during this time frame. Among these crimes, 2 million were cases of cruelty, while 1 million were cases of assault and harassment.

What is particularly disheartening is the evident correlation between the increasing number of crimes and the lack of effective preventive measures by the government. This calls for urgent attention and support for initiatives aimed at addressing and preventing crimes against women. It is clear that the government needs assistance in developing comprehensive strategies and policies to safeguard women's rights and ensure their safety.

Notably, the state of Uttar Pradesh stands out with the highest number of reported crimes, reaching a staggering 588,000 incidents. Similarly, Andhra Pradesh also witnessed a significant number of crimes against women.

Through this project, we aim to shine a light on the urgent need for societal change, enhanced legal protections, and robust support systems for women in India. By raising awareness and advocating for meaningful reforms, we can work towards a future where women feel safe, empowered, and free from the threat of violence.
