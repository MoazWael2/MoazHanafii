Key Analysis

In the first three months of opening, the supermarket's sales data analysis revealed promising results. The total profit achieved was 10% of the total revenue, indicating a successful start for the business and its ability to cover expenses. The impressive profit per customer of 15.38 further showcases the supermarket's profitability.

The analysis also uncovered some patterns in sales. Weekends and end days of the month, especially starting from the 26th, experienced lower sales. This observation suggests a potential correlation with people's salary cycles, impacting their spending behavior during those times.

Interestingly, the analysis indicated that there were no significant differences in profit or total sales based on customer types. This finding highlights the supermarket's ability to attract a diverse range of customers and generate consistent profits across different segments.

Overall, these insights showcase the supermarket's strong performance in its initial months of operation, while also providing valuable information to optimize sales strategies and address the observed sales fluctuations.
